Hmong People in My World

Cultural Identity Through Intersectionality


Hmong people through ethnicity and Culture have gone through a great dead of trauma and abuse. Despite these challenges, many Hmong people have been working endlessly to gain back what they have and what they have lost to reclaim and preserve their cultural heritage. I know this because I am hmong. The struggles like the language, tradition, and the history shape who we are as people and what we stand for. Some things like the centuries of migration and displacement from origins within southern China to the mountains of Laos, Vietnam, Thailand, and other neighboring countries have made it difficult for Hmong people to maintain their cultural traditions and identity.


The trama from the Vietnam war and the secret war brought displacement of the Hmong people. This displacement brought about by the conflict has led to a loss of connection to their roots and challenges in passing down traditions to future generations. This lead to younger generations to struggle with who they are and where they come from. I am a prime example of this not even knowing that I was hmong until I got to highschool. I felt disconnected and lost, searching for a sense of identity and belonging. From the displacement came resettling in many different places, these places consist of a various amounts of cultures and languages that further worked into displacing Hmong people from our traditions and culture. Often these places were places of war and from this war Hmong people were hunted and seen as the scum of the earth by various asian groups. Often if Hmong people didn't adapt and forced to conform into these other asian languages and cultures then you were often executed or ended up starving with no home to live in or to go to. A whole race suddenly homeless and nationless. All because of the need to colonize.

Socioeconomical and Generational Gap


The socioeconomic status of hmong people led to a difficult time after the vietnam war from losing a nationality to losing a home to becoming a refuge. Many Hmong people faced challenges adjusting to a new way of life in a foreign country, often experiencing discrimination and poverty. And although I don't know it the best I do understand the struggles that comes with it as being first generation isn't the easiest thing in the world to do especially when you see your parents struggling to be able to do much of anything to just get you your education so you can do better than them.


Being a first generation Hmong person is interesting. I get to see the difference between the newest generation aka me and the old generation that went through the war and immigrated to the U.S. The generational gap is interesting as with it brings an interesting perspective on the cultural changes and challenges faced by my family. One such gap that a lot of immigrant and first generation families have is the language gap. Although I speak English it isn't my native language and there was even a point in time where I would have to work towards translating documents like medical documents or bills for my parents so they would be able to understand and comprehend the significance of the scenario. One such bad time was having to tell my family what the doctors were saying and having to tell my family drastic information.


Another difference is the tradition that the different generations bring. If you are the older generation you were expected to go out at 15-16 and get a wife and make your own life and work in the fields and provide for your family. But even sadder is as a woman you were expected to go and get married at 14 and expected to have kids then but if you didn't there was a good chance of you getting marital kidnapped where the husband goes and takes the wife from her family. But the new generation especially the ones like me who live in the us is different. We expect to live with our parents until we move out or until we get kicked out like how it is for Americans. We expect to be treated like an American when our parents don't even know what its like to live that life. Us as the newer generation work towards creating a future where we can support ourselves and our families without relying on our parents. We work towards education to elevate hmong people and ourselves to better standings. While the older generations work for survival and for the future generations to do better than they did.


The mental health of immigrant generation brings astigmatism and a coping mechanism often resulting from the war from immigrating from refugee camps and are often left untreated leading to generational trama. While the newer generation often seek out mental health support from this generational trama but often will end up experiencing the emotional stress from balancing cultural expectations with personal aspirations.

Settler Colonialism


Settler colonialism is more personal than not for me. From the cycle of displacement to erasure of my people. This has been happening for generations upon generations. Beginnign with being forced out of china, to surviving the war in Laos to surviving the Vietnam war and finally arriving in the U.S. As refugees. Hmong people have been treated as expendable outsiders and still are. Even the first time hmong people were represented was in 2023 when hmong people were seen as veterans for fighting in the vietnam war that happened over half a century ago. The Hmong people continue to face discrimination and marginalization in society. From beintg used in the Secret War and then abandoned, forced to flee and begin again with nothing. Even here in America, Hmong people are often invisible expected to conform while our history gets erased and forgotten.


Growing up I always constantly felt pressured to succeed in a system that forces you down, a system that doesn't want you there, a system that doesn't recognize where we came from. Hmong people carry the trauma of war, colonization, and culture loss, while also attempting to preserve who we are. For us Hmong people settler colonialism isn't just in the past it's something we're still healing from now in this current age and day. Hmong people were controlled for over a century from the French and the Chinese, and the Viets, and so much more and now we just began healing and are expected to just forget about our past and conform to a world that views us as expendables.
